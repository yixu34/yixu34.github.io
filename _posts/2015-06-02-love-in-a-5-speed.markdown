I decided to be lazy and repost a poem I wrote about my Miata.  I wrote this around Valentine’s Day on 2014 in a spontaneous bout
of romantic inspiration.  Enjoy.  
  
**Love in a 5 speed**  
  
I wake up on a Saturday morning, sunshine piercing the clouds  
Birds singing, the ground still wet from last night’s rain  
I think of leaving this warm, comfortable bed and going somewhere, anywhere  
Today could be like watching every single movie I’ve ever watched  
I know I need only walk to the curb, and you will be there  
  
I look at you, with raindrops on your hood, sleeping in as you like to do on weekends  
I smile as I walk up to you.  “Wake up!”, I say.  “Let’s go for a drive!”  
You’re still groggy from your slumber, but you know this is what you were made for  
The key turns, the engine starts.  Into first gear  
Off we go, on our adventure  
  
The highway is sparse, so different from during the week!  
Hands gripping the wheel, the feeling of freedom and being one with the road  
Shift to top gear.  The engine sings like a love song  
Passing other cars like they were street signs.  We do our dance in and out of lanes  
What a nimble and beautiful partner you are  
  
I keep thinking I was supposed to go on a hike, or visit the city, or see an old friend  
But those were just excuses to be with you  
You truly make it about the journey and not the destination  
Every trip back home is just the start of longing for the next one  
  
The sun is setting and the air is chilly.  It’s been a good day, but it’s time to go home  
Your headlights flip up.  The night is so lonely and tranquil  
I feel weary, and want to return to my bed  
The exit is in sight.  Home is close!  
But I want just one last dance with you  
  
Downshift to fourth. Vroom.  
Downshift to third. Vroom!  
Heel-toe downshift to second. VROOOOOOM!!!  
I zip through the corner and your tires slip  
We laugh together, and just when you think you can’t take anymore I steer us back on course  
We’ve done this a hundred times before, but my heart still races every time  
  
We pull up to the curb  
Headlights off, engine off.  I know you want to keep going, but I’m only human  
I step out and walk back to the house, but I’m in no hurry  
I turn and look at you as I’m walking, smiling at you just as I did in the morning  
I climb into bed and close my eyes, replaying the day in my mind  
Until next time.
